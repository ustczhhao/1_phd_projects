# PhD Research Projects in Evolutionary Genetics, Mathematical Modeling, and Computational Biology

This repository collects the major research projects completed during my PhD, spanning evolutionary genetics, genome architecture, mutation-rate evolution, and quantitative computational biology. Much of the work is motivated by *Tetrahymena* as a model system for studying noncanonical inheritance, polyploidy, and facultative sex.

The projects are organized into two phases. The first three projects were completed during early PhD rotations and emphasize foundational training in stochastic simulation, quantitative analysis, and computational modeling. The later projects constitute the core of my formal PhD research and dissertation, focusing on how genome architecture, amitosis, and reproductive mode shape mutation load, adaptation, and mutation-rate evolution.

A central feature of this body of work is the integration of **mathematical modeling, algorithm development, simulation, and theoretical analysis** to study complex biological systems in a rigorous and reproducible way.

## Research Areas

This repository brings together projects addressing several connected questions in evolutionary and computational biology:

- the evolutionary consequences of amitosis, mitosis, and sex;
- the effects of genome architecture on adaptation and long-term fitness;
- the dynamics of deleterious mutation accumulation and mutation load;
- the evolution of mutation rates in dual-genome and single-genome systems;
- the use of mathematical and algorithmic frameworks to analyze biological complexity.

## Project Organization

### Early Rotation Projects (In folder "previous_code")

These projects were completed during the exploratory stage of my PhD and emphasize scientific programming, simulation design, and quantitative modeling foundations.

1. **Stochastic Simulation of Chemical Reaction Systems**  
   Implementation of exact stochastic simulation algorithms for multi-species chemical systems, including direct and first-reaction methods.

2. **Simulation of Protein Translation Dynamics**  
   Sequence-dependent stochastic modeling of ribosome movement and translation efficiency, incorporating codon usage, elongation rates, and ribosome stalling.

3. **NK Fitness Landscape Simulation**  
   Computational analysis of rugged fitness landscapes, including adaptive walks, local optima, and epistatic interactions under alternative NK model architectures.

### Dissertation Research Projects

These projects form the core of my formal PhD research and dissertation, centered on mathematical and computational analysis of genome architecture, mutation dynamics, and evolutionary processes.

4. **Amitosis Reduces Mutation Load Compared to Mitosis**  
   Analytical and numerical modeling showing that amitosis can reduce deleterious mutation load relative to mitosis under mutation–selection balance.

5. **Amitosis Slows Muller’s Ratchet**  
   Stochastic population-genetic simulations demonstrating that amitosis can slow the accumulation of deleterious mutations in finite populations.

6. **Effects of Genome Architecture on *Tetrahymena* Evolution**  
   Simulation-based analysis of how chromosome copy-number control, linkage structure, and crossover influence long-term evolutionary dynamics.

7. **Amitosis, Overdominance, and Genetic Load under Fisher’s Geometric Model**  
   Fixed-optimum mathematical modeling showing that amitosis can generate genetic load when fitness depends on maintaining intermediate allele copy numbers.

8. **Amitosis Speeds Adaptation under a Moving Optimum**  
   Extension of Fisher’s geometric model to changing environments, demonstrating that amitosis can improve adaptation under shifting selective conditions.

9. **Genome Architecture and the Evolution of Micronuclear Mutation Rates in *Tetrahymena***  
   Stochastic modeling of mutation load and mutation-rate evolution in a dual-genome ciliate system.

10. **Facultative Sex and Mutation-Rate Evolution in Single-Nucleus Systems**  
    Comparative modeling of how facultative sex, ploidy, dominance, and recombination influence indirect selection on mutation-rate modifiers.

## Mathematical and Computational Emphasis

Across these projects, I developed and applied:

- mathematical models of evolutionary systems;
- stochastic simulation algorithms;
- Wright–Fisher and Moran population-genetic frameworks;
- Fisher’s geometric model;
- NK fitness-landscape modeling;
- symbolic derivation and theoretical analysis;
- numerical simulation, parameter sweeps, and quantitative evaluation.

## Significance

Taken together, these projects examine how nonstandard inheritance systems and genome architectures influence mutation load, adaptation, drift robustness, and mutation-rate evolution. While several dissertation projects are biologically motivated by *Tetrahymena*, the broader contribution of this repository lies in its use of mathematical modeling and algorithmic analysis to address fundamental questions in evolutionary genetics and theoretical biology.

More broadly, this repository reflects PhD-level training in:

- formulating biological questions as quantitative problems;
- developing algorithms and simulation frameworks for theoretical research;
- integrating modeling, numerical analysis, and biological interpretation;
- producing rigorous and reproducible computational research outputs.
